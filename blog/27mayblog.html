27 May

227th of May is the third day of ISEL Tech.
The day will start with our friends from Oxposec, when André Batista will present interesting facts about Reverse Engineering. We will continue with privacy and data protection in open ecosystems by Rodrigo Adão and a workshop by José Sousa about web vulnerabilities. Enrique Navalon will share with us Web Apps concepts after lunch and if you were thinking why and how to code, Marco Amado, a Lead Developer at Moloni will talk with us about "Why teach your young padawans how to code." The legal impact of Articifial Intelligence in different industries and what can we expect in the coming years will be discussed by Jorge Martins, from ACEPI Digital Economy Association in Portugal and we will continue with data protection of country information and its citizens, a talk with Sergio Silva, High Council of Magistracy. One of the most expected events is the debate Cibersecurity - what, who, when? Privacy vulnerabilities nowadays - managed by Pedro Félix, Francisco Gomes, Rui Cruz Ricardo Pinto and Pedro Pinto. More than 60% of Portuguese population have internet access. The citizen is not only an user, nowadays seen more as an product. How can the population can be educated for its own protection? The involved representants from ISEL, High Council of Magistracy, Pplware, Tugaleaks & Hackers Portugal, Ministry of Internal Affairs will share with us the reality of a very actual topic: how to be secure on the internet while exists a certain lack of information and regulation regarding our protection, which are the merging challenges regarding security on the internet? You can know more about this debate here . After the debate, we'll have a SEMINAR - From the challenges of Cybersecurity to the opportunities of the RGPD (General Regulation on Data Protection). Are you with us on 27th of May?
